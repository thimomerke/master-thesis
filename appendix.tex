\section{Derivation of formulas (3.2) and (3.6)}
Derivation of equation 3.2:\hfill \break
Activation energy \(E_a\) from \textcite{wogelius_olivine_1992}:
\[
  E_a = 2.303 * R * \frac{\log_{10}R_1-\log_{10}R_2}{\frac{1}{T_2}-\frac{1}{T_1}}
\]
\[
  \Leftrightarrow \log_{10}R_1 = \frac{E_a}{2.303*R}*\left(\frac{1}{T_2}-\frac{1}{T_1}\right) + \log_{10}R_2
\]
with \(T_i\) being the temperature in Kelvin and \(R_i\) being the dissolution rate at the respective temperature. The activation energy was computed by \textcite{wogelius_olivine_1992} to be 79.5 \(\frac{kJ}{mol}\) of olivine rock, based on their own experiments and previous literature.\hfill \break
\break
Derivation of equation 3.6:
\[
  L = \int_0^{\infty}m(y)\,dy= \int_0^Y\left(1-\frac{y}{Y}\right)^3\,dy \quad\text{for } 0 \le y \le Y
\]
by substituting \(u = \frac{y}{Y}\), rewrite as
\[
  L = \int_0^1(1-u)^3\,du*Y = Y\int_0^1(1-u)^3\,du
\]
solving to
\[
  L = Y\left[-\frac{(1-u)^4}{4}\right]_{0}^{1} = Y\left[\left(-\frac{(1-1)^4}{4}\right)-\left(-\frac{(1-0)^4}{4}\right)\right] = \frac{Y}{4}
\]
